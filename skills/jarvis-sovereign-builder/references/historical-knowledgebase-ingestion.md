# Historical Knowledgebase and Full-Corpus Reconstruction

Use every lawfully available relevant source: active conversation, project instructions, uploads, archives, ChatGPT exports, source packs, request/assistant histories, ledgers, repositories, runtime/deployment evidence, approved connectors and current public research. Preserve raw content and derive normalized requirements separately.

Discovery order: current request/conversation; canonical project artifacts; uploads/archives; export reconstruction packs; source/upload/thread/requirement/build/evidence ledgers; runtime repos/deployments; approved connected stores; current official/public research. Search exact terms, synonyms, aliases, titles, message text, code symbols and module IDs.

Compose compatible archive, file-search, OCR, transcription, knowledgebase and connector skills. Do not assume exhaustive coverage without their manifests and denominator.

For every source record ID/dedupe ID, title/type/origin/owner/timestamps, size/unit count, hash or reason unavailable, raw pointer, extraction/chunk/index status, errors, links to requirements/modules/artifacts/tests/decisions, applicability and exclusion reason. Failed and pending sources remain in the denominator.

For chats preserve chronological user and assistant messages separately, conversation/project/title/timestamps/branch metadata, attachments, request-response-artifact links, corrections, approvals, rejections, unresolved loops and historical versions. For archives inventory members before safe extraction, prevent traversal/bombs, hash files, recurse within limits, record encrypted/corrupt/unsupported members, and preserve tables/code/metadata/images/boundaries.

Deduplicate at byte/hash, normalized-text, semantic and version/canonical layers without deleting raw duplicates. Preserve conflicts through DecisionRecords.

Maintain lexical, semantic, entity/relationship, chronological, requirement/implementation, provenance/citation, freshness/expiry and contradiction/decision indexes with exact pointers.

Classify findings as applicable requirement, useful pattern, implementation evidence, historical context, superseded, conflict, unsafe/out-of-scope, duplicate or unresolved gap. Assistant suggestions are not automatically approved requirements.

No-gaps requires all known sources accounted for; all extractable units extracted or failed; every chunk linked/indexed/excluded; significant requests linked to responses and requirements; applicable requirements mapped to modules, implementation/backlog, test/waiver and evidence; and discrepancy, duplicate, conflict, pending-ingest and coverage reports with disclosed denominator.

On UPDATE discover/hashes deltas, ingest only changes, refresh ledgers/registries/matrices/reports, identify new capabilities/risks/opportunities, safely integrate pre-authorized low-risk changes, and regenerate continuity/evidence packs.
