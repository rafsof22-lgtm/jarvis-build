## Sources checked

### Australian crypto, advice and records

- ASIC INFO 225: digital-asset products and services may fall within Australian financial-services law depending on their rights, features and the service being provided. Advice, dealing, custody and market operation can create licensing obligations.
- ASIC financial-advice guidance: financial advice and AFS licensee conduct are regulated; digital financial advice is an active regulatory area.
- ATO crypto recordkeeping: records are required for each crypto asset and transaction, including dates, purpose/counterparty, exchange records, AUD value, costs and wallet records. The ATO recommends regular exports and generally requires records to be retained for the applicable period.

## Jarvis requirements added

1. Recommendation outputs must classify whether they are information, general guidance, personal advice candidate or execution candidate.
2. Any comparison or recommendation capable of influencing an investment decision must pass an Australian advice-boundary classifier and professional review gate where applicable.
3. Jarvis must retain transaction, wallet, exchange, AUD valuation, cost and tax-lot evidence with immutable source pointers.
4. The CFO federation must support regular export, backup and reconstruction of exchange/wallet histories.
5. Custody, dealing, advice and execution services must be isolated and separately permissioned.
6. Marketing or presentation of crypto opportunities must include anti-misleading and evidence-sufficiency controls.

### AI governance and continuous learning

- NIST AI RMF 1.0 provides a risk-management framework for organisations designing, developing, deploying or using AI systems.
- NIST's Generative AI Profile extends the framework for generative-AI risks.
- NIST's AI Resource Center supports testing, evaluation, verification and validation practices.

## Jarvis requirements added

1. Every agent and skill must have a current-state and target-state risk profile.
2. Continuous-learning changes must be evaluated through testing, verification, validation and documented risk treatment.
3. Learning proposals require provenance, evaluation data, failure cases, security review, approval, canary evidence and rollback.
4. Agent/model changes must record affected users, systems, decisions, data and legal boundaries.
5. The economic-engine recommendation system requires a dedicated risk profile covering financial harm, misleading output, data quality, automation bias, privacy, model drift and unauthorised action.
